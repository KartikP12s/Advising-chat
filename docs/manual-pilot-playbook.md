# Manual pilot playbook

## Objective

Learn whether pseudonymous peer conversations are useful and whether participants understand and trust the optional reuse workflow. The first pilot validates behavior and operations; it does not validate automatic matching, sanitization, moderation, or media generation.

## Before recruiting

- Complete production privacy/security/legal readiness or conduct sessions as clearly labeled moderated research without entering sensitive data into the prototype.
- Define eligible topics and exclusions. Do not launch high-risk medical, legal, crisis, or financial categories without qualified safety design.
- Train the facilitator, safety lead, privacy reviewer, and editor; name an on-call owner.
- Prepare participant information, research consent, compensation terms, retention/deletion dates, withdrawal channel, crisis/escalation resources, and incident form.
- Test an authorization/deletion drill and a published-asset takedown drill.
- Use synthetic records in the app unless the persistence layer and controls have passed review.

## First 20 conversations

| Phase | Sessions | Operation | Decision signal |
| --- | ---: | --- | --- |
| Moderated usability | 1–5 | Facilitator observes both sides; text-only; no public content | Participants can complete matching/chat/reflection and explain privacy limits |
| Concierge matching | 6–10 | Operator manually verifies topic fit and schedules a peer | Match acceptance and completion are plausible; safety workload is manageable |
| Consent rehearsal | 11–15 | Eligible participants may walk through transformation consent using synthetic/demo text | ≥90% explain that value and consent are separate; declines feel consequence-free |
| Manual candidate pilot | 16–20 | At most a few mutually approved sanitized scripts become private draft storyboards | Review effort, revision count, and privacy risk are measurable and acceptable |

Do not set a quota for consent or content candidates. An entirely private session is a successful product outcome.

## Session SOP

1. Confirm participant eligibility and emergency limitations; remind them not to share names, exact locations, employers, account details, or third-party secrets.
2. Create pseudonymous aliases and verify each person can privately access only their own session.
3. Explain match reasons as heuristics, not a guarantee of expertise or safety.
4. Start the text session; facilitator remains outside content unless a disclosed moderated-research protocol says otherwise.
5. Monitor system health and report queue, not message bodies. Use exceptional access only for a specific safety trigger.
6. End the session and collect sealed feedback independently.
7. Reveal only the mutual gate result. Do not disclose who declined or either person’s written answer.
8. If eligible, run the full transformation and exact-version approval workflow without persuasion.
9. Record operational metrics and delete data on schedule.

## Content-candidate SOP

1. Verify both transformation consents are current and compatible.
2. Produce a local sanitized draft; log every redaction category.
3. Complete a human indirect-identifier and safety review.
4. Send the same immutable version to both participants.
5. Apply requested changes as a new version and invalidate prior approvals.
6. After both approve, create a text-led private storyboard only.
7. If editing changes meaning, title, emphasis, or wording, return for participant review.
8. Have a separate authorized operator release the asset.
9. Record all first-party/vendor locations so withdrawal can trigger complete takedown.

## Measurement sheet

Capture aggregate or coded data where possible:

- Match offered/accepted/skipped and reason category.
- Time to match, session start/completion, message count, reconnect/send failures.
- Each participant’s private value outcome; mutual value as a derived boolean.
- Helpful-moment count without copying message text into analytics.
- Report/block events, triage time, and outcome.
- Consent comprehension result, decline/withdrawal state, and time spent deciding.
- Sanitization categories, human review minutes, revisions, approval time.
- Candidate/render/editor cost, publication decision, and takedown completion time.

Never optimize by pressuring for a higher consent conversion rate. The meaningful funnel is conversation usefulness; content reuse is a separate trust and operations measure.

## Stop conditions

Pause the pilot immediately for unauthorized data access, a missed withdrawal/takedown, a credible safety incident without adequate response, repeated participant confusion about reuse, sanitization that exposes a person, or a vendor/data flow outside the disclosed boundary. Preserve only the minimum evidence necessary for incident handling, notify the accountable owner, and do not resume until remediation is verified.

## Weekly review

Review funnel and reliability aggregates, safety cases, privacy misses, consent comprehension, deletion completion, operator burden, and participant feedback. Decide one of: continue unchanged, narrow topics, change the interaction, strengthen operations, or pause. A small sample supports product learning, not claims about population outcomes.
