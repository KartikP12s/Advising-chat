# Future roadmap

The roadmap is evidence-gated. Each phase earns the next by proving usefulness, safety, comprehension, and operational control—not by maximizing feature count.

## Now: prototype and research

- Run the current deterministic, zero-provider demo.
- Conduct accessibility, privacy comprehension, and matching usability studies with synthetic scenarios.
- Execute five moderated text sessions under an approved research protocol.
- Measure the full lifecycle, especially sealed feedback, revisions, and withdrawal.
- Keep media as a non-published text-led storyboard.

Exit evidence: people can complete the journey, understand limits, decline without pressure, and operators can close safety/deletion requests.

## Next: production text pilot

- Add real authentication/session handling and separate identity/product schemas.
- Add PostgreSQL persistence, row-level authorization, encrypted fields, backups, and tested retention/deletion jobs.
- Add private realtime messaging with idempotent sends, reconnection, rate limits, and abuse controls.
- Build dedicated safety and sanitized-content workspaces with audited exceptional access.
- Implement append-only consent and immutable version approvals.
- Add privacy-safe aggregate telemetry, budget alerts, and operational runbooks.

Exit evidence: authorization and deletion tests pass, incident/takedown drills work, pilot value is repeatable, and human review capacity is known.

## Later: controlled content operations

- Introduce a locked sanitized-script export and asset inventory.
- Produce the first assets manually with abstract visuals and neutral narration.
- Compare Shotstack/Creatomate-style rendering only after a repeatable template exists.
- Test whether synthetic presenters improve comprehension enough to justify added privacy and perception risk.
- Add caption, disclosure, licensing, and participant re-review checks.

Exit evidence: participants approve the final framing, privacy misses are acceptably rare and recoverable, takedowns are verified, and unit economics use measured labor.

## Explore only after evidence

- Advisor scheduling and availability tooling.
- Additional languages with culturally appropriate safety and moderation.
- Topic-specific advisor training and boundaries.
- Audio conversation with separate recording consent and retention.
- 1:2 or small-group advising with a redesigned multi-party consent/version model.
- Compensated advising, which adds identity, tax, fraud, and marketplace obligations.

Potential partner shapes—each a separate discovery and governance track—include verified specialists, universities and career services, professional coaches, community organizations, service-provider referrals, and broader resource networks. Longer-term product surfaces could include topic-based groups, a searchable library of mutually approved insights, multilingual sessions, partner APIs, and paid expert sessions. None should reuse the pilot’s safety, identity, matching, or consent assumptions without revalidation.

## Explicit non-goals

- Public social feed or virality loop.
- Participant popularity scores or competitive rankings.
- Automatic publication.
- Voice or likeness cloning of participants.
- AI-generated medical, legal, mental-health, or financial authority.
- Expansion into new formats before text safety and privacy operations work.

## Decision metrics

At each gate review completed and mutually valuable conversations, match acceptance, reliability, reports, privacy misses, consent comprehension, revision cycles, withdrawals, review time, deletion/takedown completion, and total cost. Growth is not a valid override for a safety or consent regression.
