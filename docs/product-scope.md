# Product scope

## Hypothesis

People will have more candid and useful advice conversations when identity performance is minimized, the match is understandable, and any public reuse happens only after the conversation proves valuable and both participants retain control.

The prototype tests two business stages:

1. Match and private conversation.
2. Value signal and mutual opt-in, followed by a believable review workflow.

## Primary users

- **Seeker:** needs relevant lived perspective without creating a public post.
- **Peer advisor:** volunteers experience without becoming a public personality or being positioned as a licensed professional.
- **Research/content operator:** evaluates aggregate funnel health and sanitized candidates without default access to raw messages.

## In scope

- Anonymous session identity and minimal onboarding
- Deterministic matching with visible reasons
- Reliable text-first conversation simulation
- Helpful moments, reactions, safety controls, end-session action
- Independent staged feedback
- Mutual value and transformation-interest gates
- Specific, versioned transformation consent
- Local sanitization and participant redaction
- Exact-version approval and withdrawal
- Static media storyboard and editable cost projections
- Seeded internal dashboard and research states

## Explicitly out of scope

- Public social feed, followers, or reputation scores
- Payments and paid expert sessions
- Production authentication or persistent realtime messaging
- Audio/video calls, recording, transcription, voice cloning, or likeness generation
- Production AI matching, safety classification, sanitization, or media rendering
- Native mobile apps, group conversations, or 1:2 sessions
- Automatic publication

## Product principles

1. The conversation is the product; content is a possible later artifact.
2. A useful outcome does not imply permission to reuse it.
3. Consent has a purpose, policy version, content version, actor, and timestamp.
4. Decline and withdrawal must be visible, neutral, and consequence-free.
5. Raw and sanitized content live in different access domains.
6. Explain the match; do not market an unvalidated compatibility oracle.
7. Never make a participant’s sensitive story appear to be spoken by them.

## Success measures for a pilot

| Measure | Why it matters | First learning target, not a promise |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Match acceptance | Relevance and comfort | >60% |
| Completed conversation | Operational viability | >65% of accepted matches |
| Mutually valuable | Core value | >45% of completions |
| Mutual transformation interest | Content supply without pressure | 5–20% of completions |
| Sanitization first-pass approval | Privacy quality | >50% |
| Revision cycles | Review burden | median ≤2 |
| Withdrawal before publication | Trust signal, not a failure | measure without minimizing |
| Participant comprehension | Ethical validity | ≥90% pass plain-language check |

All dashboard rates are currently labeled as seeded assumptions and must not be presented as measured traction.
